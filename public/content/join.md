## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can international students apply?**  
A: Yes! We welcome applications from talented researchers worldwide. We provide visa sponsorship for qualified candidates.

**Q: Is remote work possible for internships?**  
A: Hybrid and remote arrangements can be discussed on a case-by-case basis. We prefer in-person collaboration but are flexible based on circumstances.

**Q: What is the expected publication outcome?**  
A: We aim for top-tier venue publications (ICML, NeurIPS, ICLR, CVPR, etc.). Most PhD students publish 2-3 papers during their studies.

**Q: Do you accept undergraduate researchers?**  
A: Yes, exceptional undergraduate students can join as visiting researchers or summer interns. Please include a strong recommendation letter from your advisor.

**Q: What programming languages and frameworks do you use?**  
A: Primarily Python with PyTorch. Familiarity with distributed computing frameworks (Ray, Horovod) is a plus.

**Q: Are there teaching responsibilities for PhD students?**  
A: PhD students typically serve as teaching assistants for 1-2 quarters per year, which provides valuable teaching experience and additional funding.
